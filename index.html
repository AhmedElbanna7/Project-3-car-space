Step inside and explore a world where every car is more than just a ride – it’s an experience built for your journey.

the most famous models in our showroom

Sedans are four-door cars designed to combine comfort, style, and practicality. They offer roomy interiors and smooth handling for everyday use. Fuel-efficient and reliable, they are perfect for city and highway driving. From family cars to luxury models, sedans remain a timeless favorite.

Sports cars are built for speed, style, and thrilling performance. With powerful engines, sharp handling, and sleek designs, they offer an exciting driving experience and a strong road presence. From timeless classics to modern supercars, sports cars deliver pure driving pleasure for enthusiasts who crave excitement.

Electric cars are powered entirely by electricity, offering a clean and eco-friendly alternative to traditional vehicles. They deliver instant acceleration, quiet performance, and lower running costs. With advanced technology and growing charging networks, they are becoming more practical every day. Electric cars represent the future of driving, combining innovation with sustainability.

showroom news

bmw

mon, may 25th 2020

BMW is pushing forward with new models, led by the Neue Klasse iX3, an electric SUV offering over 800 km of range and ultra-fast charging, set to launch in Europe in 2026. A new i3 electric sedan, positioned as a 3 Series EV, is also in development. The i4 gains efficiency upgrades and a powerful M60 xDrive trim with 601 hp, while the 2 Series Gran Coupé gets refreshed variants in India. Across its lineup, BMW is adding features like heated seats and the Digital Key, with plans to launch 40+ new models by 2027.

bugatti

mon, may 25th 2020

Bugatti is entering a new era with the Tourbillon, a 1,800 hp hybrid V16 hypercar replacing the Chiron, limited to 250 units with production starting in 2026. The brand also launched the Mistral, the fastest open-top Bugatti at 282 mph, with all 99 units sold. Under its new Programme Solitaire, Bugatti revealed the Brouillard, a bespoke coach-built model, while demand has filled production slots through 2029.

tesla

mon, may 25th 2020

Tesla is updating its lineup with the new Model Y L in China, a longer six-seater SUV now in deliveries. It has started building its first affordable EVs, aiming for mass production later in 2025. The Cybertruck RWD has been dropped, leaving only AWD and Cyberbeast trims. Meanwhile, Tesla is expanding its robotaxi trials in Arizona, targeting half of the U.S. market by year’s end.
